Introduction

In September, Daimler Trucks North America briefed us on their endeavour to create a system of autonomous trucks with a connected platform, Mission Control. This challenge in innovation raised numerous questions in human-machine and human-computer interaction.We have researched, analyzed, and designed a solution we believe to be novel and valuable to the problem space.

Research

We began our research with the intent to understand the current trucking industry today and how autonomy might affect the current industry.

Our research was guided by the following questions in mind:
How do DTNA’s customers’ businesses operate? Who are the main stakeholders?
What are the implications of moving to vehicle autonomy? How will this affect the drivers and other stakeholders?

To help answer these questions, we conducted generative research methods including desk research and interviews with experts and users. This stage produced several key findings.

Using sensemaking and problem framing methods, these findings helped us produce insights and implications into the problem space.

Insight

The flow of a trucking mission can be modeled into three main phases: pre-mission, intra-mission, and post-mission.

Implication

The Mission Control platform should be designed to solve user needs within these phases.

Problem Framing

The most influential research insight was how the driver is heavily involved in communicating their status and their decisions. The human driver played an integral role in planning the trip; they are responsible for ensuring the truck is safe to operate, that the cargo is properly stored in the trailer, and that the roads are safe to drive. Without a human driver, we therefore have a gap between the dispatchers and planners and the actual truck’s condition. In the case of an unexpected event, the dispatcher is forced to piece the puzzle together, there’s no onsite human to figure out and discuss solutions.

How might we connect the human stakeholders, especially dispatchers, to what’s happening in the autonomous trucks when there is no human present to report on it?

Ideation

We used our generative research insights to make several sketches of potential solutions, and using the evaluative research method of speed dating we got feedback from drivers and industry experts.

Solution

For our solution we have created Vidi, mission control’s ubiquitous conversational AI that you can talk to and also receive information from. Conversational agents today like Siri and Alexa are quite limited in their abilities to hold a conversation. We propose Daimler work toward a conversational agent that does more than answer yes or no questions.
